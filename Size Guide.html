Get Started

How to Measure Your Feet

  1. Gather a piece of paper, a pen, and a ruler, then stand on the paper with your heel against a wall.
  2. Mark the longest part of your foot (from heel to the tip of your longest toe) with the pen.
  3. Measure the distance between the wall and the mark in centimeters or inches using the ruler.
  4. Repeat the process for the other foot, as one foot may be slightly larger than the other.
  5. Use the larger measurement to find your size in the charts below.

Tips for the Perfect Fit

  • Measure your feet in the evening, as they tend to swell slightly throughout the day.
  • Consider the type of socks you’ll wear with your shoes—thicker socks may require a slightly larger size.
  • If you have wider feet, opt for a half size up to ensure enough room in the toe box.
  • Walk around in your shoes on a clean surface to test the fit before wearing them outdoors.
  • Check that there’s about a thumb’s width of space between your longest toe and the end of the shoe.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I’m between sizes?
We recommend sizing up for a more comfortable fit, especially if you have wider feet or plan to wear thicker socks.
How often should I measure my kids’ feet?
Kids’ feet grow quickly, so we suggest measuring every 2-3 months to ensure the best fit.
Do your shoes run true to size?
Most of our shoes run true to size, but we recommend checking the size chart and measuring your feet for accuracy.
Can I return shoes if the size doesn’t fit?
Yes, we offer a hassle-free return policy within 30 days if the shoes are unworn and in their original condition.
